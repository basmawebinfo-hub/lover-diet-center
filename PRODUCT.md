# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Arabic and English-speaking individuals seeking weight loss, body sculpting, and healthy eating plans. They want professional, science-backed guidance delivered with warmth and simplicity. Context: browsing on mobile or desktop, often in the evening or between meals, looking for trust before committing to a plan.

## Product Purpose

LoverDiet helps people transform their bodies and eating habits through expert nutritional consultations, healthy meal plans, snack products, body sculpting sessions, and training courses. Success means users feel supported, see measurable results, and trust the brand enough to return and refer others.

## Brand Personality

Warm · Expert · Motivating

Like a personal trainer who genuinely cares: authoritative but never cold, encouraging but never cheesy. The tone is knowledgeable and direct, with a human warmth that cuts through the noise of generic health apps.

## Anti-references

- Hospital-clean aesthetics (sterile white/blue, clinical vibes)
- Aggressive bodybuilding imagery (extreme physiques, gym-bro tone)
- Fake luxury / over-designed interfaces (excessive gradients, glassmorphism, decorative bloat)
- Generic SaaS templates (the hero-metric cliché, identical card grids)

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through professionalism.** Every visual element should reinforce credibility — clean spacing, deliberate typography, polished micro-interactions. Teal as the anchor color signals expertise; orange as the accent signals action without desperation.
2. **Warmth without weight.** The interface should feel human and approachable without being chatty or decorative. Photographs of real food and real people, not illustrations or abstract art.
3. **Bilingual by default, not afterthought.** Arabic and English share equal weight. RTL layout, typography, and spacing must feel native in both directions, not mirrored.
4. **Results speak louder than features.** Surface outcomes (before/after, testimonials, measurable progress) before explaining how the product works. The interface is a window into transformation, not a feature list.
5. **Progressive commitment, never a wall.** Users should explore, learn, and build trust before being asked to register or pay. The onboarding flow is a conversation, not a form.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Full RTL support for Arabic. Semantic HTML throughout. Keyboard-navigable with visible focus indicators. Color contrast ratios that hold up in both light and dark conditions (teal on white, orange on white, white on teal/orange). Support for screen readers in both languages.
